    விழிக்குத் துணைதிரு மென்மலர்ப் பாதங்கள் மெய்ம்மைகுன்றா..

    23 May, 2019 Kandhar Alangarm


    விழிக்குத் துணைதிரு மென்மலர்ப் பாதங்கள் மெய்ம்மைகுன்றா
    மொழிக்குத் துணைமுரு காவெனு நாமங்கள் முன்புசெய்த
    பழிக்குத் துணையவன் பன்னிரு தோளும் பயந்ததனி
    வழிக்குத் துணைவடி வேலுஞ்செங் கோடன் மயூரமுமே


    vizhikkuth thuNai thiru menmalarp pAdhangkaL, meimai kundRA
    mozhikkuth thuNai murugA enum nAmangkaL, munbuseidha
    pazhikkuth thuNai avan panniru thOLum, bayandha thani
    vazhikkuth thuNai vadivElum sengkOdan mayUramumE.

    Back Ground

    This poem is one of the 108 stanzas from Kandhar Alankaram; alankaram in tamil means decoration; Arunagirinathar decorated the Tamil God Murugan the embodiment of love and beauty with meticulately weaved piece of poems; these poem aesthetically narrates about the Lord Murugan's beauty, his nature, his forbearance with our sins and also about how his vel and peacock guard us in our course of life.

    It is important for us to manage our thought process to seek positivity always, many theory claims that you become as your thoughts, when we talk about thoughts it is not just about thinking, it is about perception, perception is a thought process involved with belief or faith. To have this perception right for us to prosper, the poet tells us to surrender the foot of his holiness Lord Murugan who only can give us the consciousness;
    Along with perception, it is our choice of words we use would also partake as a factor in determining our fate. It is our words that finds a place of respect and trust in others heart, it is our words that teach others, so we will have to chose words that uplifts the human kind. To command those words effectively, the poet tells the devotees to keep chanting the Almighty's name like kandha, kadamba, karthikeya, muruga and some among of his indefinitive names.
    In Thirupugazh, Arunagirinadhar compares the number of lives we had with the number of sands in seven seas, thats a scary comparison. So how would we be able to break the loop of this life cycle; unless we get rid of our karma that ensue us in this dead loop, we will not be able to attain the salvation; Arunagirinadhar tells us to get hold of the Lords shoulder to swim across this ocean of birth and to attain his feet.
    We might be surrounded with family and friends who would take part in our ebb and flows of life; It is certain that we would leave this world one day or the other; it is also certain that we had to travel alone leaving our fellow members behind; what would follow us is just the reactions of our karma, we are not sure about the path we would take and where it would lead us to, in order to protect us from the frightning path, the poet tells us to get seek help from the Mighty Vela and Magnificient Peacock to guard us.

    A beautiful poem, that tells us the way to lead a peaceful life. Araharohara !!
